Events

August 6th

Sangeet and Garba 5PM - 11PM

Among the many pre-wedding events that occur prior to South Asian wedding ceremonies, the Sangeet is where the party begins. Friends and family perform songs and choreographed dances for the bride and groom as part of the wedding celebrations. It'll be a fun and crazy night full of dancing!

Garba is a form of circular dance that originated in Gujarat (where Ankur's family is from). People dance around the center in a circle making sweeping motions with their arms. This routine is followed by Dandhiya Raas, which is a structured dance with wooden sticks. We'll have a little intro and tutorial so you can join in! You can also prepare by watching this video.

August 7th

Haldi 10AM-11AM

For the haldi ceremony, everyone gets to "paint" the bride and groom with a turmeric (haldi) paste. The Haldi ceremony is a ritual holy bath also known as pithi ceremony, which is one of the pre-wedding ceremonies in India. Turmeric, oil and water are applied to both the bride and groom by married women on the morning of the wedding. The mixture is believed to bless the couple before the wedding.

  Dress code

For The Culture

Sangeet and Garba

Cultural Cocktail Wear
Traditional Indian fashion is typical for a Sangeet and Garba night. Wear something you can dance in, or be prepared to take your shoes off so you can dance. Please also feel free to wear something traditional from your culture!
Traditional Indian attire includes sarees, chaniya choli (crop top and skirt), pyjamas, and kurta suits.

Haldi

Casual Attire
Since Haldi is a less formal ceremony and will only involve a few people, please wear whatever you want. Some people may dress up in traditional Indian wear. Feel free to join the, but you can also wear light colors and/or casual attire.

Wedding

Cultural Glam
This is the time to shine! Wear a saree, a chaniya choli, a suit, a kurta, or any formal traditional wear from your culture. Check out this Pinterest board for inspiration.
